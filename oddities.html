There have been many oddities concerning shiny hunting throughout the franchise.

Gen 2

In gen 2, glitches were still abundant, and while some of them were patched out with the new games, quite a few still remained. One of my favorite glitches, and the one we will be talking about is the cloning glitch. This glitch would allow you to receive a (near) infinite amount of odd eggs as long as you continued to repeat the glitch over and over again.

Gen 3

In gen 3, specifically Ruby, Sapphire, and Emerald, the RNG was broken in such a way that each trainer id and secret id combination was mapped to a specific array of frames. This table could, with the proper setup, guarantee specific pokemon, and even the shininess of said pokemon. Of course, this requires some rather extensive setup, as you have to find both the trainer id and secret id of your save file, and find a program that can take that combination and convert it into the array of frames.

Gen 4

In gen 4, there was an exploit that went overlooked by the devs, this was the cute charm glitch. This glitch is quite complex and can take some time to fully understand, but at its core, it requires the user to have a very low TID and SID, or, have a specific combination of TID and SID, and have a pokemon with cute charm at the lead of your party when encountering a wild pokemon.
